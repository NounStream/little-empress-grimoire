⏱ 30–60 min

Through Homebrew, RetroArch brings ancestral games back — NES, SNES, Sega Genesis, Game Boy — colorful, simple worlds perfectly suited to a young child's attention span and motor development.

  1. On computer: download RetroArch for Wii from retroarch.com → Platforms → Nintendo Wii.
  2. Extract download. Copy the retroarch folder to /apps/ on your SD card (create apps if it does not exist).
  3. Insert SD card into Wii. Open Homebrew Channel → select RetroArch → press A to launch.
  4. On first launch: Online Updater → Update Cores. Download: NESTOPIA (NES), SNES9x (SNES), Genesis Plus GX (Sega), mGBA (Game Boy).
  5. Place ROM files on SD card: /roms/nes/, /roms/snes/, etc.
  6. In RetroArch: Load Content → browse to your ROM → select a core when prompted → play.
⚠ Legal Note — ROMs and Your Conscience Only use ROMs for games you physically own. Do not download pirated ROMs — this is both illegal and unnecessary, as many classic games are re-available legally via Nintendo Switch Online. The guiding principle: if you own the cartridge, you may dump it. If you do not own it, do not use it.   1. From Wii menu: select the Wii button (bottom-left circle) → Wii Settings.

If you have forgotten the PIN, contact Nintendo Support with your console serial number — they can provide a master key based on the serial number and the current date. Important: Nintendo Wii support availability varies by region and may change over time; check your local Nintendo support site for current options. Keep your Wii serial number noted somewhere safe and separate from the device.

⏱ 20–30 min

Part A — Screen Time (the outer wall):

  1. Settings → Screen Time → Turn On Screen Time.
  2. Tap "This is My Child's iPad" — creates a child-profile passcode separate from the device PIN.
  3. Set Downtime: tap Downtime, enable it, choose hours (e.g., every day from 20:00 to 08:00). During downtime, only calls and Always Allowed apps work.
  4. Set App Limits: tap App Limits → Add Limit → Entertainment → set 30 or 60 min/day.
  5. Under Always Allowed: add your 4–5 chosen apps by name. Remove all others from this list.
  6. Set a Screen Time Passcode — different from your device PIN. Write it in a private, secure place.
⚠ If You Forget Your Screen Time Passcode Go to Settings → Screen Time → Change Screen Time Passcode → Forgot Passcode? and authenticate with your Apple ID. If your Apple ID is also inaccessible, contact Apple Support directly. Keep your Apple ID credentials stored somewhere safe and separate from the device.

Part B — Guided Access (the magic circle):

  1. Settings → Accessibility → Guided Access → toggle On.
  2. Passcode Settings → Set Guided Access Passcode. Use a different code from Screen Time.
  3. Optionally: under Time Limits, set a session duration (e.g., 15 min) so sessions end automatically.
  4. To start: open chosen app → triple-click the side button (Face ID iPad) or Home button (older iPads) → tap Start.
  5. Child is now locked in one world only — she cannot navigate away.
  6. To end: triple-click → enter Guided Access passcode → tap End.

🚨 If Something Feels Wrong — Signs to Pause and Reassess

The Kalunga boundary is sacred — but if it is not holding, that is important information. Watch for these signals and use them as an invitation to adjust limits, not to feel guilt.

  • ▸She resists ending the session every time — tears, negotiation, or anger at the moment the Kalunga closes. Adjust the closing ritual first; if it persists, shorten sessions by 3–5 minutes.
  • ▸She asks for the iPad outside the designated ritual context — at meals, before sleep, or first thing in the morning. Return firmly and warmly to the daily schedule you wrote together. The boundary holds.
  • ▸Sibling conflict or family tension intensifies visibly around the device. This may signal the ritual is not "co-play" in practice — review whether screens have drifted toward passive, solo use.
  • ▸Real-world play or physical activity decreases noticeably. The screen is a bridge, not a destination — reinforce the Real-World Echo after every session.
  • ▸Your own comfort with the ritual drops — you are skipping sessions, shortening co-play, or handing the device without ceremony. This is the most important signal. The Kalunga weakens when the guardian is tired. Rest, then return.

Any of these signals: talk together, adjust limits, and return to the Kalunga image — the boundary is sacred, and it is yours to redraw at any time.

🔄 How to Swap a Language Slot — 3 Steps for Any Family
  1. 1 Choose which slot changes. For example: Slot 4 (Guardian's Choice) from Italian → Dutch. Slot 1 (Heritage) should only change if your family's heritage language changes — protect it.
  2. 2 Find one app that supports the new language — check its Settings for an audio language toggle. Many apps (Khan Academy Kids, Smart Tales) support 5+ languages from a single settings menu. Replace one app, not all at once.
  3. 3 Update your Daily Sacred Circle opening phrase — add the new language to your naming sequence during sessions. Introduce it gently: "We have a new word today — in Dutch, the lion is leeuw." Repetition across 5–7 sessions builds it in.
To add a fifth language: Find apps that support it, and above all — speak it during sessions. No app replaces an adult voice in a child's heritage language.
